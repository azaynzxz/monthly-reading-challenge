I am perched on a tiny blue plastic stool on a sidewalk in the Old Quarter of Hanoi, and this is my regular breakfast spot. Above me, the tangled electrical wires look like a messy bird's nest, and a Banyan tree pushes its roots through the cracked pavement nearby. The lady who runs this stall has been serving Pho for over twenty years, and her broth smells wonderfully of star anise and cinnamon, which are the essential spices in Vietnamese cooking. Motorcycles buzz past just inches from my elbow, and the noise is constant. However, when I am focused on this savory and steaming soup with its soft rice noodles and tender beef slices, I find my calm in the middle of the chaos. This simple bowl of Pho is how millions of Vietnamese people start their day, and I now understand why it is considered the national dish of this beautiful country.
