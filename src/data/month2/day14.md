I am walking slowly through the famous Sunday Walking Street market in Chiang Mai, which is the largest and most ancient city in northern Thailand. The sun has set behind the mountains that surround this valley, and the old city walls made of red brick are beautifully lit up by strings of twinkling lights. The air is thick with fragrant smoke from dozens of grills cooking all kinds of delicious street food, and the smell is so wonderful that it makes my stomach growl with hunger. I find a small spot to sit on the curb near the old moat, which is a water channel that has protected this city for over seven hundred years since it was founded. In my hand is a stick of grilled meatballs that I bought for just a few coins from a friendly vendor, and the spicy dipping sauce is perfectly balanced between sweet, sour, and hot flavors. Nearby, traditional musicians are playing ancient instruments including the khim, which is a hammered dulcimer that produces haunting melodies. It is loud, hot, and crowded with both tourists and locals, but I absolutely love the energy of this place. The night markets of Thailand are more than just places to buy things because they are social gatherings where communities come together to eat, talk, and enjoy the cool evening air after the heat of the tropical day.
