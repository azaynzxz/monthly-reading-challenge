I am sitting inside an old tram that is rattling through the busy streets of Kolkata. This city is the only place in India where trams still run, and they have been operating since 1902. The tram moves slowly past the grand colonial buildings and the famous Howrah Bridge. Outside the window, I can see street vendors selling puchka, which is a crispy snack filled with spicy water. The sounds of the city mix with the clanging of the tram bell. Kolkata was once the capital of British India, and you can still feel the history in every corner of this beautiful and chaotic city.
