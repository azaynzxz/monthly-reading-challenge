I am pushing my way through the crowded and noisy lanes of Chandni Chowk in Old Delhi, and this market has been operating continuously for over 350 years since the Mughal era. It is incredibly loud here because rickshaws, scooters, and thousands of people are everywhere, and they are all moving in different directions at once. I am standing near an old spice shop that smells strongly of cumin, turmeric, and dried red chili, and the powerful aroma makes my nose tickle. I am here to buy fresh vegetables for my family, and the farmer shouts the price of his spinach while I shout back a lower price because bargaining is a game that both sellers and buyers enjoy playing. The narrow lanes were originally designed for horse carriages, and they have not changed much since Emperor Shah Jahan built this market in the seventeenth century. As I carry my heavy bags home through the afternoon heat, I feel proud that I am feeding my family with fresh food from one of the oldest and most famous markets in all of Asia.
