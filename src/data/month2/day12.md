I am standing in the courtyard of Al-Aqsa Mosque in Jerusalem, which is one of the holiest sites in Islam and the place where Prophet Muhammad began his miraculous night journey called Isra' wal Mi'raj. The golden Dome of the Rock shines brilliantly under the stars, and the ancient stone walls seem to whisper stories from centuries past. Tonight is the 27th of Rajab in the Islamic calendar, and Muslims around the world are commemorating this sacred event that happened over 1,400 years ago. According to Islamic tradition, the Prophet was transported from Mecca to Jerusalem in a single night on a heavenly creature called Buraq, which means lightning because of its incredible speed. From the rock inside this magnificent dome, he then ascended through the seven heavens, meeting previous prophets including Ibrahim, Musa, and Isa, and he witnessed the wonders of paradise and received the command for Muslims to pray five times daily. I am here with my family, and we are listening to the imam tell the story with deep reverence and emotion. The cool night air carries the scent of incense and olive trees from the surrounding hills. This journey represents the spiritual connection between earth and heaven, and it reminds us that faith can transcend all physical boundaries and limitations.
