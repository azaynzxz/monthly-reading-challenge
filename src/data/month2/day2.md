I am walking through the busy morning market in Parakou, which is a vibrant city in northern Benin. The sun is already warm, and the air smells of fresh yams and roasted peanuts. Parakou is known as the commercial heart of the north because traders come from many villages to sell their goods here. I stop at a stall run by a woman from the Bariba tribe, and she is selling colorful woven baskets. The sounds of bargaining and laughter fill the air. I buy some fresh mangoes for breakfast, and the sweet juice drips down my chin as I walk home through the red dust streets.
