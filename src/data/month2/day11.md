I am waking up slowly on a traditional houseboat floating on the peaceful waters of Dal Lake in Kashmir, which is often called Paradise on Earth because of its stunning natural beauty. The morning mist is rising from the still water, and I can see the snow-capped Himalayan peaks reflected perfectly on the glassy surface of the lake. These wooden houseboats have been a unique part of Kashmiri culture for over a century, and each one is beautifully carved and decorated with intricate patterns that showcase the exceptional craftsmanship of local artisans. I hear a gentle splash outside my window, and I look out to see a shikara, which is the traditional Kashmiri gondola, gliding past with a flower seller offering fresh lotus blooms and marigolds. The shikarawala, which is what we call the boatman, is singing a soft Kashmiri folk song as he paddles. Later this morning, I will take my own shikara ride through the floating vegetable gardens where farmers grow tomatoes, cucumbers, and melons on beds of woven reeds that float on the water. The air is cool and fresh, and it smells of pine trees from the mountains and wood smoke from the kitchens on other houseboats. Living on the water like this feels like being in a completely different world, far removed from the noise and chaos of modern cities.
