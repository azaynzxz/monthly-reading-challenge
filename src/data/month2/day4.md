I am sitting on a colorful tiled rooftop in the middle of the Fez Medina. Below me, the narrow streets form a maze, and I can smell the leather tanneries from here. Up on the rooftop, it is peaceful. Serving tea is an art form in Morocco, and I carefully put fresh mint leaves and plenty of sugar into the silver pot. When the tea is ready, I lift the pot high above the glass and pour slowly, and I try not to spill a single drop. The goal is to create a thick foam, and this sweet and hot drink is our daily pause from the busy world below.
