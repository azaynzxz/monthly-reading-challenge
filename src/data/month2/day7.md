I am standing near the ferry terminal in Eminonu, which is one of the busiest and most historic districts of Istanbul, and I am watching the boats cross the famous Bosphorus strait that separates Europe from Asia. Across the shimmering water, the Galata Tower stands tall like a stone finger pointing at the cloudy morning sky. I hear the seller before I see him because he is shouting his familiar call of fresh Simit for sale. He carries a tower of sesame bread rings balanced perfectly on his head, which is a skill that takes years to master. I buy one for just a few coins, and it is wonderfully crunchy on the outside yet soft and chewy on the inside. Tearing off pieces of this traditional bread while dodging the seagulls who desperately want a bite has become my favorite morning ritual in this ancient city that straddles two continents.
