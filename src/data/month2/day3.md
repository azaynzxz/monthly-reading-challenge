I am leaning against the warm clay wall of our community tandoor in the Panjshir Valley. The mountains around us are high and capped with snow, and they look like sleeping giants in the distance. We do not buy bread from a factory because we make it right in this deep clay oven instead. I watch the women slap the dough against the hot walls with their bare hands, and I wonder how they can handle such heat. In minutes, the bread puffs up and becomes golden and bubbling, and the smell of fresh naan drifting through the cold air is the best perfume in the world.
