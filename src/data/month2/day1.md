I am standing on a dusty street corner in Lahore, and I am right under the shade of an old Neem tree. Every evening, we turn this street into our stadium. We use a tennis ball wrapped in electrical tape, which we call a tape ball because it swings more. I bowl fast and aim for the plastic crate we use as a wicket. The game stops when a rickshaw passes and honks loudly, but nobody minds the interruption. This is our daily ritual of laughter and pure passion for cricket.
