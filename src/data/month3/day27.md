I am sitting in a circle with my neighbors in Ouagadougou, under a big Baobab tree. The evening is cooling down after a hot day. Someone starts playing a djembe drum, and others join in with different drums. Boom, boom, pat, pat, boom! The rhythm is infectious, and soon people are dancing. I am not a great drummer, but I try to keep the beat. Children run around us, laughing. This music brings our community together. We don't need a concert hall; we just need drums, rhythm, and each other. The music continues until late into the night.
