I am standing at Circular Quay in Sydney, and the view in front of me feels like something from a dream. To my left, the Sydney Opera House rises from the edge of the water. Its roof is made of enormous white shells, covered in over one million ceramic tiles that catch the sunlight and glow. The Danish architect Jorn Utzon designed it to look like sails on the harbor, and it truly does. To my right, the Sydney Harbour Bridge stretches across the water in a massive steel arch, so tall that it reaches 134 meters above sea level. Locals call it the Coathanger because of its shape. Ferries glide underneath it while tourists climb its arch for views of the whole city. Standing here between these two landmarks, I feel the magic of what humans can create when imagination meets the sea. This is architecture that makes the ordinary world feel extraordinary.
