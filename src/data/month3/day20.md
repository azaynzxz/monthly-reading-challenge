I am sitting at a small plastic table on a sidewalk in Managua, under a big Ceiba tree. The morning is already hot. In front of me is a plate of Gallo Pinto, rice and beans mixed together with onions and spices. It comes with a fried egg on top and a piece of fried plantain. I eat it with a spoon, mixing everything together. The flavors are simple but perfect. This is what we eat every morning, and it gives us energy for the day. A good breakfast doesn't need to be fancy, just honest and filling.
