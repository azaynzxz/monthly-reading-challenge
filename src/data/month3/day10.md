I am wading through muddy rice paddies on the outskirts of Antananarivo, my feet sinking into the soft earth. The green rice plants are tall and swaying in the breeze. I can see other farmers working in the distance, their backs bent under the sun. We are planting new seedlings today. My back is starting to ache, but I keep going. This rice will feed our families for months. A heron stands perfectly still in the water nearby, waiting for fish. Working the land connects me to my ancestors who did the same work.
