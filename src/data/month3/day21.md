I am walking alongside a camel caravan in the Sahara, near Nouakchott. The camels move slowly, their feet sinking into the sand. The sun is intense, and I wrap my headscarf tighter. We are carrying salt and dates to trade. The desert stretches out endlessly, just sand and sky. At night, we will make a fire and sleep under the stars. The silence here is profound, broken only by the sound of camel bells, jingle, jingle. This ancient way of travel connects me to traders who crossed this desert centuries ago.
