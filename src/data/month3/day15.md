I am standing by a river outside Port Moresby, helping to process sago palm. The tall palm trees sway above us, their leaves rustling in the wind. We cut down the trunk and pound the pith to extract the starch. It is hard, sweaty work! The white paste will become our staple food. My arms are tired, but everyone is working together, laughing and talking. This traditional method has been used for generations. Eating food we made ourselves from the land makes it taste better, somehow.
