I am sitting at a small metal table on a sidewalk in Tirana, near an old Plane tree. The morning traffic is loud, but here it feels calm. I am drinking Turkish coffee, thick and strong, from a tiny cup. The coffee grounds settle at the bottom. My friend reads my fortune from the leftover grounds, turning the cup upside down. It is all in fun, but we believe it anyway! This slow coffee ritual is how we start our day. We don't rush; we sit and talk. Time moves differently here.
