I am sitting inside our family yurt high in the Tien Shan mountains, near a small stream. The round felt tent is warm and cozy. Outside, I can see horses grazing on the green grass. My grandmother is making tea on a small stove in the center. The smoke rises through the opening at the top. We sit on colorful rugs, sharing stories. Life here is simple but rich. At night, we will sleep under thick blankets, listening to the wind. This traditional home connects us to our nomadic ancestors.
