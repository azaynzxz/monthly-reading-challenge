I am walking through the narrow lanes of Chandni Chowk, which is one of the oldest markets in Delhi. The air is thick with the smell of hot oil and sugary syrup. A vendor is frying bright orange spirals of Jalebi in a huge pan. He squeezes the batter through a cloth, making perfect circles in the bubbling oil. I take one and bite into it. It is crunchy on the outside and soaked in warm sugar syrup on the inside. Then I move to a Pani Puri stall, where the seller cracks open a crispy hollow ball and fills it with spicy tamarind water and mashed potatoes. I put the whole thing in my mouth at once because that is the only proper way to eat it. The burst of sour, spicy, and sweet flavors all at once makes my eyes water, but I immediately ask for another one.
