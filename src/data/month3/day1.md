I am walking through the Pasar Beringharjo market in Yogyakarta, right next to an old Banyan tree that has been here for generations. The morning air is already warm and sticky. I can hear the sound of vendors calling out prices, their voices mixing with the clatter of motorbikes. I stop at a small stall to buy fried tempeh. The seller hands it to me, still warm from the pan. I eat it standing there, watching people rush by with their baskets full of vegetables. This simple breakfast connects me to my city's heartbeat.
