I am kneeling on the sidewalk near Shwedagon Pagoda in Yangon, just before sunrise. The golden pagoda towers above us, catching the first light. I have prepared a small bowl of rice and curry. Soon, the monks in their saffron robes will walk past in a silent line. I place the food into their bowls as they pass. They do not speak, but their eyes show gratitude. This daily ritual reminds me that giving is as important as receiving. The quiet morning feels sacred and peaceful.
