I am helping my grandmother in her kitchen in Yerevan, making lavash bread. We roll out the dough until it is paper-thin, then she slaps it against the hot walls of a tonir, a clay oven in the ground. The bread cooks in seconds, puffing up and turning golden. The smell fills the whole house! We stack the warm bread in a pile. Later, we will eat it with cheese and herbs. Making bread this way is an art, and my grandmother has been doing it for sixty years. This tradition connects me to my heritage and my family.
