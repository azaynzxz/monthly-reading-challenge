I am riding through the streets of Old Dhaka in a cycle rickshaw, and the whole vehicle is a work of art. The back panel is painted with a scene of a Bollywood hero standing in front of a waterfall, surrounded by bright flowers and green parrots. The tin mudguards have paintings of the Royal Bengal Tiger, which is the national animal of Bangladesh. Even the seat has a velvet cover with golden tassels hanging from it. Dhaka is called the Rickshaw Capital of the World because nearly 800,000 of these three-wheeled vehicles fill its streets. Each one is decorated differently by local artists who use house paint and simple brushes to create these moving masterpieces. The art style is called Rickshaw Art, and it has been a tradition since the 1950s. As we weave through honking traffic and narrow alleys, I realize that these painted rickshaws transform the chaos of Dhaka into a traveling open-air gallery.
