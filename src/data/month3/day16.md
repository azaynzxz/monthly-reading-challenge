I am waiting in line outside the neighborhood bakery in the medina of Tunis, holding my dough wrapped in a cloth. The old stone building has a huge wood-fired oven inside. I can feel the heat from here! The baker takes my dough and marks it with my family's symbol. Soon, my bread will come out golden and puffy. The smell of fresh bread fills the whole street. Everyone here knows each other, and we chat while waiting. This shared oven brings our neighborhood together. Nothing tastes better than bread baked this way.
