I am sitting on a mat on the sidewalk in Luang Prabang, just before dawn. The street is quiet except for the sound of my own breathing. I have prepared sticky rice in small baskets. Soon, the monks will walk past in their orange robes, carrying their bowls. I will place a small ball of rice into each bowl as they pass silently. The ritual is peaceful and meditative. This daily practice teaches me about giving without expecting anything back. The morning light makes everything glow softly.
