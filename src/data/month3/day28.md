I am sitting on a mat in a nakamal, a traditional meeting house in Port Vila, surrounded by friends. The evening is warm and humid. We are sharing kava, a drink made from the root of a pepper plant. It tastes earthy and slightly bitter, and it makes my tongue tingle a bit! We drink it from coconut shells, passing them around. The conversation flows slowly and peacefully. This ritual has been part of our culture for generations. Drinking kava together creates a bond between us. It is more than a drink; it is a way of connecting.
