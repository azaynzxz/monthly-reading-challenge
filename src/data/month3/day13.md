I am sitting on the beach in Maputo, under the shade of a Casuarina tree, mending a fishing net. The ocean waves crash nearby, and I can taste salt in the air. My fingers work the needle and twine, repairing the holes. This net belongs to my father, and it has caught fish for our family for years. The work is repetitive, but it requires focus. A fisherman walks by and gives me advice. When I finish, the net will be strong again. This simple skill connects me to the sea and my community.
