I am picking coffee cherries on a hillside in the mountains of East Timor, my basket hanging from my shoulder. The coffee plants are small trees, covered in red and green berries. I pluck them carefully, choosing only the ripe ones. My fingers are getting stained red! In the distance, I can see the ocean, a blue line on the horizon. Later, we will dry and roast these beans. Coffee is our main crop here, and it supports many families. Working in these hills makes me feel connected to the land and my community.
