I am standing at a small street stall in San Salvador, watching the cook make pupusas. She takes a ball of masa dough and stuffs it with beans and cheese, then pats it flat between her hands. Clap, clap, clap! She cooks it on a hot griddle until it is golden and crispy. The smell makes my stomach growl! I order two, and she hands them to me wrapped in paper. I eat them standing there, adding spicy curtido from a jar. This simple food is our national treasure, and it costs almost nothing.
