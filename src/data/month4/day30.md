I tightly gripped the thick, freezing rope, looking down at the incredibly deep, dark crevasse, which is a very deep, dangerous crack in glacial ice. I was guiding a small, highly determined group of amateur hikers through a treacherous, extremely dangerous mountain pass high in the snowy, breathtaking Himalayas. A fierce, completely unexpected blizzard had caught us forcefully off guard, deeply burying our safe, established trial in freezing, massive snowdrifts. We blindly fought through the roaring, aggressive wind, tying ourselves securely together to prevent anyone from accidentally falling off the steep, completely invisible cliffs. The incredibly low, freezing temperatures rapidly drained the batteries in our essential GPS devices, leaving us completely lost in a massive, terrifying world of solid, blinding white. We desperately huddled tightly together inside a shallow, frozen ice cave, rationing our tiny supply of cold food and desperately trying to stay warmly awake. A massive, terrifying avalanche suddenly cascaded violently down the sharp mountain face directly above us, sealing the small opening of our icy cave tightly with heavy, compacted snow. My tired lungs desperately gasped for the rapidly dwindling, thin air, and sheer, absolute terror completely paralyzed my freezing, exhausted limbs. I quickly grabbed a small, metal climbing shovel and frantically began digging aggressively through the solid, freezing wall of packed snow. After an hour of agonizing, exhausting labor, we successfully broke through the icy barrier, finally allowing the fresh, freezing mountain air to flood beautifully back into our dark, suffocating trap. We slowly navigated safely back to the warm, bright base camp the following, sunny morning, deeply grateful simply to be alive. That terrifying, very close brush with complete disaster firmly cemented my deep belief that intense, focused teamwork and sheer, stubborn willpower are the greatest, most necessary tools for ultimate survival.
