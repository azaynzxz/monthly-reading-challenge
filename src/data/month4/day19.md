I tightened my colorful, woven poncho against the freezing, biting wind that swept furiously across the high, rugged peaks of the Andes Mountains. My remote, quiet village was suffering from a strange, unknown illness, and traditional, modern medicines had completely failed to cure my sick neighbors. I volunteered to climb to the very top of the dangerous mountain to find the legendary, wise elder who supposedly knew all ancient, secret herbal remedies. I hiked slowly up the steep, rocky trails, carefully gasping for thin air at the incredibly high, dizzying altitudes. I successfully navigated across narrow, terrifying rope bridges that swayed violently over incredibly deep, dark river gorges, which are very deep, narrow valleys between mountains. I eventually reached a small, hidden stone hut surrounded by strange, glowing mountain plants and brightly colored, exotic flowers. A sudden, fierce snowstorm erupted without warning, completely covering my only safe path back home in thick, freezing white snow. My fingers grew painfully numb, and my teeth chattered uncontrollably as the freezing, heavy wind battered the tiny, fragile stone hut for hours. The old, wrinkled elder kindly offered me a warm, highly bitter tea made from the rare, magical herbs I desperately needed. He carefully taught me exactly how to mix the powerful medicine, and we waited patiently together by the warm fire until the furious storm finally passed. I safely returned to my sick village and immediately administered the powerful, ancient remedy, watching my friends and family slowly recover their health. The difficult, freezing journey beautifully taught me that ancient, traditional wisdom is sometimes the only reliable answer to our modern problems.
