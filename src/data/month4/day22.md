I wrapped my thick, wool scarf tightly around my freezing neck, staring in absolute awe at the massive, twisting structure of the Great Wall of China. I had traveled halfway across the crowded, busy world to hike along these ancient, crumbling stones and connect with the incredible, profound history they represented. I eagerly began my steep upward climb, determined to reach the highest, most distant watchtower before the sun fully set behind the dark, jagged mountains. I hiked for hours along the uneven, rocky steps, carefully stepping over ancient, moss-covered bricks and admiring the breathtaking, vast valleys below. A thick, confusing fog rolled rapidly in, completely obscuring my view and making the treacherous, steep stairs dangerously slippery. I suddenly realized that I was completely alone on a remote, isolated section of the massive wall, separated entirely from my original tour group. The dense, grey fog thickened rapidly, and an eerie, complete silence descended over the ancient, winding path, amplifying the sound of my own anxious, rapid breathing. My knees shook uncontrollably, and cold sweat formed quickly on my cold face as I desperately tried to find any recognizable landmark in the blinding, thick mist. I stopped moving entirely and forced myself to take several deep, calming breaths, carefully listening to the distant, faint sound of a bronze bell ringing from the correct, main pathway. I cautiously followed the rhythmic, metallic sound, eventually reuniting with relieved, frantic guides who had been actively searching the cloudy mountainside for me. I finally stood at the highest, majestic watchtower, appreciating the incredible, ancient structure with a new, profound sense of deep respect. The terrifying, disorienting fog taught me that even the greatest, most massive human achievements can easily be completely swallowed by unpredictable nature.
