Back home, tea is not merely a simple drink, but it is a warm way of welcoming people into your life and a universal sign of hospitality. Today, I sat at a busy roadside cafe and watched the chai-wala, who is the master tea maker, perform his craft. He poured the hot, milky tea from a great height into the cup, stretching the stream to make it perfectly frothy and bubbly. The rich, aromatic smell of cardamom and cinnamon filled the air and cut right through the dust of the street. As I held the warm clay cup in my hands, I realized that taking this brief break to connect with friends is actually the most important part of my day.
