I took the rotating cable car up to the flat top of the mountain that overlooks Cape Town. Below me, the city met the deep blue ocean. The top of the mountain was not bare, but instead, it was covered in fynbos, which is a unique type of vegetation found nowhere else on earth. As I stood there, balanced between the sky and the sea on one of the oldest mountains in the world, I felt like I was on top of everything while watching the clouds roll by beneath my feet.
