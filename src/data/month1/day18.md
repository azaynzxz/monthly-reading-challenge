Summer in this region is unbearably hot, and the relentless sun beats down on everything with an intensity that makes you want to stay indoors. However, I willingly forgive the extreme heat every single year because it brings the wonderful gift of mangoes, which we proudly call the King of Fruits. My father came home this afternoon carrying a large wooden crate of Anwar Ratol mangoes, and he also brought some Chaunsa and Sindhri varieties, which are equally famous for their unique flavors and textures. These prized mangoes are often used by diplomats and officials as gifts to build friendships and strengthen international relations with other countries. He placed the crate on the kitchen floor, and the sweet fragrance immediately filled the entire house. We did not bother slicing them properly with knives and plates because that would take away from the authentic experience. Instead, we practiced aam chusna, which is the traditional way of softening the mango in your hands and then sucking out the pulp directly, getting sticky golden juice absolutely everywhere on our fingers and faces. It was gloriously messy and incredibly sweet, and we laughed together as the juice dripped down our chins. This is the authentic taste of pure summer happiness, and it is a tradition that brings my family closer together every year when the weather turns warm and the mango trees begin to bear their precious fruit.
