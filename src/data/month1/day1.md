I woke up long before the sun rose today because the excitement made it impossible to sleep. The morning air was remarkably cool and misty as I began to climb the damp stone steps of the ancient temple. I could truly feel the weight of history beneath my feet with every step I took. When I finally reached the top, I waited in silence and listened intently as the jungle below began to wake up. Slowly, the sky transformed into a soft purple hue, and then it blossomed into a blazing orange that illuminated the bell-shaped stupas. These famous dome-like structures have stood here like silent guardians since the ninth century. It is incredible to think that this massive monument, which is the largest Buddhist temple in the world, was constructed without a single drop of cement. As I gazed out over the vast green canopy, I felt a profound sense of peace. It is the kind of deep stillness that you simply cannot find in the busy and noisy city that I left behind.
