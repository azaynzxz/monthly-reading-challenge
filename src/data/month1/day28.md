I traveled high up into the towering Karakoram mountains along the famous Karakoram Highway, which is often called the Eighth Wonder of the World because it is the highest paved international road on Earth. My destination was the legendary Hunza Valley, which is often called a real-life Shangri-La because the people who live here are rumored to enjoy extraordinarily long and healthy lives. The journey itself was challenging and took many hours on winding mountain roads, but the breathtaking views of snow-capped peaks like Rakaposhi and Ultar made every moment worthwhile. When I finally arrived in the main town of Karimabad, I was immediately struck by the warm hospitality of the local people called Hunzukuts, who speak a unique language called Burushaski that is unrelated to any other language in the world. I visited the ancient Baltit Fort, which sits high above the valley like a guardian watching over the terraced fields below. The locals smile often and walk with an incredible spring in their step that suggests boundless energy, and this is true not only for the young people but also for the elderly, who seem to possess a youthful vitality that defies their age. They eat a simple diet of fresh apricots, walnuts, and whole grains that they grow themselves on the terraced hillsides, and they breathe the cleanest mountain air I have ever tasted in my entire life. The valley feels like a secret paradise that has been hidden away in the high peaks, far from the stress and pollution of the modern world below. Life moves at a much slower pace here, and perhaps this relaxed approach to life and their deep connection to nature and community is the true secret to their legendary long and happy lives.
