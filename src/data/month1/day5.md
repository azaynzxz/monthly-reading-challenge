I took a boat to a remote and rugged island, which is the only place on Earth where these prehistoric creatures live naturally. As I walked through the dry and crunchy forest, my guide instructed me to stay absolutely quiet. Suddenly, I saw it: a massive Komodo dragon resting in the shade, looking like a terrifying monitor lizard from a forgotten time. Its tongue flicked in and out as it tasted the air. Seeing the world's largest lizard in the wild reminded me that we share this earth with incredible, ancient beings that demand our respect.
