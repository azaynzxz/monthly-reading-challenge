The wind was absolutely perfect that afternoon because it was strong yet steady, providing just enough force to stay in control. I raced up the dusty hill with my paper kite, eager to join the Gudiparan Bazi, which is the Afghan kite-fighting game that honors both skill and freedom. The string dug into my taped fingers as I pulled, and the sky above filled with a riot of colors as paper diamonds clashed and danced in the breeze. When my kite finally sliced through my opponent's line, the cheer from the neighborhood kids rang out like victory drums, lifting my spirits higher than the kite itself. In that instant, I felt like a true champion, not just because I had won, but because I was part of something bigger. It was a game that carries pride, joy, and community in every flight. It is one of those moments of pure happiness when nothing else matters except the pull of the wind in your hands and the endless sky above.
