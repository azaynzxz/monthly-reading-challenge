I went to a lively wedding today where the food was the main topic of conversation, and it all centered around the famous 'Jollof Wars' between West African nations. The huge iron pot of rice was bright red and spicy, steaming with an inviting heat that made everyone hungry. My friend from Ghana argued passionately that their version is better, but I just smiled and took another heaping bite. The smoky flavor of the rice, which was cooked slowly over firewood to give it that distinct taste, told me everything I needed to know. It was not just food, but rather it was a delicious victory.
