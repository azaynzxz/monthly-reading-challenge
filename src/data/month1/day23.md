I was walking through the massive Alamgiri Gate of the historic Shahi Qila, which means Royal Fort in Urdu, and I found myself imagining all the powerful emperors and elegant queens who used to live within these ancient walls centuries ago. This magnificent fort sits in the cultural heart of Lahore, right next to the grand Badshahi Mosque, which was once the largest mosque in the world. The fort complex is enormous, and it contains many different buildings and courtyards that were constructed over several hundred years by different Mughal rulers. The most magnificent building inside the complex is the Sheesh Mahal, which is also called the Palace of Mirrors because its walls and ceilings are covered in thousands of tiny mirrors and colorful mosaics that sparkle brilliantly like stars in the night sky when candlelight hits them. Another stunning structure is the Naulakha Pavilion, which got its name because it cost nine lakh rupees to build, an enormous fortune at that time. As I walked through the cool marble corridors and touched the smooth polished surfaces of the ancient walls, I felt like a time traveler who had stepped back into a grand and royal past filled with luxury and splendor. The craftsmanship that went into creating this incredible place is simply breathtaking, and it serves as a powerful reminder of the rich cultural heritage that we must preserve for future generations to appreciate and learn from.
