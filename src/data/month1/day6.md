I stood at the base of the Great Pyramid, which is the only surviving wonder of the ancient world, and I felt incredibly small. The stones are massive, appearing way bigger than they look in pictures, and each one weighs as much as a car. I tried to imagine the thousands of workers who built this thousands of years ago without any modern machines, effectively creating a bridge between the ancient and modern worlds. As I touched the rough and weathered limestone, I felt connected to a civilization that understood mathematics and the stars better than I ever will.
